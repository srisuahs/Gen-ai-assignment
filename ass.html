Part A: The Mirror Journal

You are the authority here, not the AI. Document the conversation and your honest response to it.

Step 1: Choose Your Belief

Select an arguable proposition (not just a preference) that you genuinely hold and are willing to have challenged.

Too Vague (Do Not Use)
  • "I believe technology is useful."
  • "Education in India needs reform."
  • "AI is changing the world."
Specific Enough (Good)
  • "I believe learning to code from YouTube alone produces worse engineers..."
  • "AI assistants make junior engineers intellectually lazy in ways visible after 2 years."

The Seven Mirror Sessions

1
Day 1 • Zero-Shot Prompting

State your belief to the AI plainly with no context or examples. Get its raw response.

Reflection: What does the gap between your meaning and the AI's response say about your clarity?

2
Day 2 • Role Prompting

Prompt the AI as a rigorous skeptic, then as the strongest defender of your belief.

Reflection: Which role was more useful and what does it reveal about your belief?

3
Day 3 • Few-Shot Prompting

Provide 3 concrete examples from your own life to test if personal context improves analysis.

Reflection: Did you choose examples that test your belief, or just confirm it?

4
Day 4 • Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Force step-by-step reasoning. Identify one unjustified leap/flaw and challenge it in a follow-up.

Reflection: Did catching the flaw require domain knowledge or just logic?

5
Day 5 • Self-Consistency

Run the best CoT prompt 5 times. Apply majority-vote principle to conclusions.

Reflection: Does AI consistency mean correctness or just confident error?

6
Day 6 • Metacognitive Prompting

Ask AI to identify where it is least confident. Audit uncertainties against your knowledge.

Reflection: Where is the boundary between AI reasoning and lived experience?

7
Day 7 • Synthesis Report

Write 500-700 words addressing belief status, technique ranking, AI as a mirror, and remaining questions.

No bullet points. Continuous prose only.

Part B: Technical Practicum

Apply the techniques learned in Part A to technical tasks. Structured, replicable formats.

B1: Prompting Basics

Days 1-2

Compare Zero-shot, Instruction, and Few-shot prompts on one moderate task.

Deliverable:

Prompts, outputs, 150-200 word comparison.

B2: Design Principles

Days 2-3

Redesign B1's weakest prompt using constraints, format specs, and negative constraints.

Deliverable:

Before/After prompts & outputs, 200w reflection.

B3: Chain-of-Thought

Days 3-4

Compare Direct Answer, open CoT, and Structured CoT on multi-step reasoning.

Deliverable:

Outputs, 250w analysis on structured vs open CoT.

B4: Self-Consistency

Days 4-5

Run the CoT prompt from B3 five times. Apply majority vote.

Deliverable:

5 outputs, final answer, 1 paragraph variance analysis.

B5: RAG

Days 5-6

Ask a factual question plain, then grounded in a text excerpt. (+5 Bonus for Python FAISS).

Deliverable:

Comparisons, 200w explanation of RAG modes.

B6: Limitations of LLMs

Days 6-7

Deliberately provoke a hallucination, a bias, and context degradation.

Deliverable:

Evidence for each, 1 paragraph per limitation.

B7: Model Comparison

Days 7-8

Design a challenging prompt and run it across at least 3 frontier models (e.g., GPT-4o, Claude 3.5, Gemini 1.5, DeepSeek).

Deliverable:

Comparison table (accuracy, reasoning, conciseness, failures) and 300w analysis.

B8: OpenAI API Implementation

Days 8-10

You must programmatically interact with the model using Python.

T1: Basic Prompt. Run a prompt of choice.
T2: CoT via API. Add a system message: "You are a careful reasoner. Think step by step."
T3: Self-Consistency via API. Write a loop to run the same prompt 5 times and print outputs.
T4: Temperature Experiment. Run at 0.0, 0.5, and 1.0.

API Key Safety: Never include your actual API key in your submitted report. Replace it with "YOUR_API_KEY" before submitting. Failure results in a -10 mark penalty.
